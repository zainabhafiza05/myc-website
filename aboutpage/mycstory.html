Our Story

Why MYC Exists

Muslimah Youth for Change (MYC) began with a simple realization. What started as time spent together by the MYC founders gradually became something far more meaningful. During our most formative years, being in one another’s company offered not only enjoyment, but also positive companionship, a nurturing Muslim environment, and opportunities for learning and growth.

As our friendships deepened, we became increasingly aware of a gap within our community: there were few spaces where Muslim girls, particularly those who wore hijab, could participate in athletics and outdoor activities with confidence and comfort. We did not believe that faith, modesty, and physical activity were in opposition to one another. In response, we launched a summer soccer program in 2017 to demonstrate that strength, confidence, and an active lifestyle could coexist seamlessly with a strong Islamic identity.

Over time, we recognized that short-term programming alone was not enough. The youth we served began facing the same questions and challenges we ourselves had encountered only a few years earlier. It became clear that what they needed were intentional, consistent spaces to return to for guidance, reflection, and meaningful connection. They needed a community they could rely on for support, growth, and belonging.

In response to this need, Muslimah Youth for Change was formally established in 2021. What began as a grassroots initiative evolved into a sustained platform dedicated to supporting young Muslimahs. Through MYC, we provide mentorship, foster sisterhood, and create opportunities for leadership development, personal growth, and community engagement, all while remaining firmly rooted in Islamic values.

Our experiences have shown us the power of good company, inspiring role models, and a meaningful community — and we are committed to giving back by nurturing the next generation of confident, ambitious, and faith-centered Muslim women Insha Allah.
